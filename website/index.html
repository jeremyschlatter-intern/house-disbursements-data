About This Project

What is the Statement of Disbursements?

The Statement of Disbursements of the House is a quarterly report published by the Chief Administrative Officer of the U.S. House of Representatives. It documents every expenditure made by the House, including payments from Members' Representational Allowances (MRA), committee budgets, leadership offices, and administrative operations.

These reports have been published for over 50 years. Since 2009, they have been available as downloadable PDFs from house.gov. Beginning in Q1 2016, the House also publishes the data as CSV files.

Data Sources

  • 2016–2025: Official CSV files published by the House Chief Administrative Officer
  • 2009–2015: Born-digital PDF reports available from house.gov (parseable with the ProPublica parser)
  • 1970–2008: Scanned PDFs digitized by the Boston Public Library and available on the Internet Archive

Schema

Each record in the unified dataset includes:

  • Organization — The office making the disbursement (e.g., "OFFICE OF THE SPEAKER", "HON. NANCY PELOSI")
  • Program — Budget program (e.g., "MEMBERS REPRESENTATIONAL ALLOWANCE")
  • Category — Spending category (Personnel Compensation, Travel, Equipment, etc.)
  • Transaction Date — Date the transaction was recorded
  • Vendor Name — The payee or vendor
  • Start/End Date — Performance period of the service/goods
  • Description — Purpose of the expenditure
  • Amount — Dollar amount

Data Notes

  • The CSV format published by the House changed over time: 12 columns in 2016–2022, expanding to 18 columns from Q4 2022 onward (adding organization codes, vendor IDs, and budget object codes).
  • Fiscal year references include prefixes like "FY" and "LY" (Legislative Year) in newer data. These have been normalized to plain years in our aggregations.
  • Some transactions have negative amounts, representing refunds or adjustments.
  • The dataset includes both DETAIL records (individual transactions) and SUBTOTAL/GRAND TOTAL rows. Our analyses use only DETAIL records.

Historical Data (Pre-2016)

The older reports (1970–2008) exist as scanned PDFs on the Internet Archive, digitized by the Boston Public Library with support from the Kahle/Austin Foundation. The OCR quality of these scans varies significantly, making automated extraction challenging.

The Sunlight Foundation / ProPublica parser (originally by Luke Rosiak, improved by James Turk) can extract structured data from the 2009–2015 text-based PDFs from house.gov. This pipeline could be extended to cover those years as well.

Prior Work

This project builds on decades of work by government transparency advocates:

  • Sunlight Foundation — Original PDF parser and House Staff Directory
  • ProPublica — Maintained and published the parser and expenditure data
  • Daniel Schuman / Demand Progress — Advocacy for structured disbursement data
  • Congressional Data Coalition — Push for machine-readable congressional data

Technical Details

The data pipeline downloads all available CSVs from house.gov, normalizes the varying column formats into a unified schema, stores everything in a SQLite database, and generates the JSON data files and CSV exports served by this website. The complete source code is available on GitHub.
